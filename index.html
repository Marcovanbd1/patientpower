MyScribe
Your appointment. Your record. Your words. Free · Forever · For patients

MyScribe is a free ambient scribe for patients. It works across every important medical appointment in your life.

A GP review. A cancer clinic. A specialist consultation. A cardiology appointment. A memory assessment. Any room where something important is decided about your health.

"Do you mind if I record this, just for my own records?" That is all it takes.

The professional says yes. You tap record. You have your appointment. You tap stop.

Who needs this

Think about who is in that room.

🩺
Long term conditions Someone managing Type 2 diabetes sees a GP, a diabetic nurse, a dietitian, a podiatrist, and a pharmacist. Every appointment builds on the last. If the chain of understanding breaks anywhere, the whole pathway suffers.
🎗
Cancer pathway Someone on a cancer pathway sees more professionals in six months than most people see in a decade. Every conversation carries weight. And they are often frightened, which makes remembering harder.
🧠
Dementia Someone with early dementia needs a record not just for themselves but for the people around them. A carer. A family member. Someone who was not in the room.
💛
Mental health Someone leaving a mental health appointment overwhelmed deserves a plain-language record of what was said, what was agreed, and where they can go next.

Every one of these people deserves an After Note.

The appointment ends. The After Note begins.
After Notes

Not a transcript.
A guide out of the room.

What just happened.
What was agreed.
What you do next.
Where to get more help.

Plain language. Before they leave the building. Designed entirely for the person who was in that room. The After Note is a new document type. As recognisable, in time, as a prescription or a referral letter. Written for the patient, not the system.

Here is what one looks like.

After Note Diabetes Review · Dr Patel · 4 July 2026
What just happened You had your annual diabetes review with Dr Patel. Your HbA1c has come down from 64 to 57 since January. That is real progress. Your blood pressure was 138/84, slightly high. Your weight was 94kg.
What did we agree Dr Patel is increasing your Metformin from 500mg twice a day to 500mg three times a day, with food. She is also starting you on Ramipril 2.5mg once a day for your blood pressure. Your new prescriptions will be ready at the pharmacy by tomorrow afternoon.
What do I do next Book a blood test in six weeks at the Riverside Health Centre phlebotomy clinic. You can book online at dorset.nhs.uk/bloodtests or call 01305 820000. Dr Patel needs these results before your next appointment. Your next review is booked for 14 January 2027 at 10.20am with the diabetic nurse, Emma Clarke.
Where can I get more help Diabetes UK have a free helpline: 0345 123 2399. Your local peer support group meets every third Tuesday at Weymouth Library. Details at thelocalsocial.org.

Nothing for completeness. Everything for utility.

The model

Free forever.
One penny.

The employer of the professional pays one penny per After Note. The GP practice. The hospital trust. The local authority. The law firm. One penny, and their patient leaves knowing exactly what was said and what happens next.
