What is an After Note

Same consultation. Two documents. Written for different readers.

Every consultation already produces a clinical note, written for the record. An After Note is generated from that same note, at that same moment, but written for the patient in plain language, covering what to take, when to worry, and what happens next.

It sits alongside the letter, the fit note, and the referral as one more option on the dispatch screen, not a new step in the workflow.

Clinical record and patient message, from a single consultation

Respiratory review, worsening symptoms, travelling on Sunday.

CLINICAL NOTE Written for the record

History

Follow up for worsening breathing. Pre existing respiratory condition. Travelling to the Caribbean on Sunday, concerned about the trip. No red flag symptoms.

Medications

Foster inhaler, carbocisteine.

Examination

Mild wheeze and a few crackles noted.

Diagnosis

Worsening breathing.

Plan

Antibiotics twice daily for seven days. Oral steroids once daily for five days, with food. Continue existing medications.

Safety netting

If not better by Sunday, book Thursday or Friday. If symptoms worsen, return.

AFTER NOTE Written for the patient

Your breathing, and what to do before Sunday

You have been prescribed a short course of antibiotics and steroids to help improve your breathing over the next few days.

What to take

Antibiotics

Twice a day for 7 days, with breakfast and evening meal.

Steroids

Once a day for 5 days, with breakfast. Take with food.

Usual inhalers

Continue as normal, alongside these.

Before you travel: this treatment aims to improve your breathing before your trip. If you are not improving by Sunday, contact the surgery before travelling.

Get medical help sooner if

  • your breathing gets worse, or you feel short of breath at rest
  • you develop chest pain, or feel significantly more unwell or confused
  • you are struggling to speak in full sentences

Why now

Patient communication is moving faster than the systems built to hold it

A lot of new investment is going into this space right now, tools that generate patient facing summaries, automated messaging, ambient documentation. Some of it works well. Most of it sits outside the clinical record entirely, disconnected from the note that was actually written.

The systems that already hold the record are in the strongest position to do this properly, generated from the same consultation, inside the same system, without a second tool for practices to manage.
